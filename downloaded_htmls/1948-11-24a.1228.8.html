Oral Answers to Questions — Hong Kong (Chinese Communists)

– in the House of Commons at 12:00 am on 24 November 1948.

Sir Fitzroy Maclean , Lancaster 12:00, 24 November 1948

asked the Secretary of State for the Colonies what steps have been taken to prevent the use of Hong Kong by Chinese Communists as a safe base for their activities against the Chinese Government.

  • Link to this speech In context Individually
  • Tweet Share
  • (Citation: HC Deb, 24 November 1948, c1228)

Mr Arthur Jones , Shipley

The activities of the Chinese Communists in Hong Kong are closely watched and deportation proceed- ings are in train in several cases where it has been found that Communists were attempting to use Hong Kong as a base for activities against the Chinese Government.

  • Link to this speech In context Individually
  • Tweet Share
  • (Citation: HC Deb, 24 November 1948, c1228)

Mr Ronald Ross , County Londonderry

What steps are taken by the Government of Hong Kong to check the entry of people from the mainland of China into Hong Kong?

  • Link to this speech In context Individually
  • Tweet Share
  • (Citation: HC Deb, 24 November 1948, c1229)

Mr Arthur Jones , Shipley

That is another question, and a very difficult one. If the hon. Member will put it down, I shall be very glad to answer it.

  • Link to this speech In context Individually
  • Tweet Share
  • (Citation: HC Deb, 24 November 1948, c1229)

Mr John Paton , Norwich

Will my right hon. Friend see that the greatest care is exercised not to restrict the right of asylum for Chinese liberals and democrats as well as Communists so long as they conform to the law of the Colony?

  • Link to this speech In context Individually
  • Tweet Share
  • (Citation: HC Deb, 24 November 1948, c1229)

Mr Arthur Jones , Shipley

indicated assent.

  • Link to this speech In context Individually
  • Tweet Share
  • (Citation: HC Deb, 24 November 1948, c1229)

Mr David Gammans , Hornsey

In view of the fact that the Government were obviously caught napping over a similar situation in Malaya, can the right hon. Gentleman assure the House that they will not be caught napping in Hong Kong?

  • Link to this speech In context Individually
  • Tweet Share
  • (Citation: HC Deb, 24 November 1948, c1229)

Mr Arthur Jones , Shipley

I do not at all accept the insinuation that the Government were caught napping. I would most completely repudiate the suggestion. So far as Hong Kong is concerned, the problem is very much before us, and I do not wish to add to the difficulties by making a statement now.
