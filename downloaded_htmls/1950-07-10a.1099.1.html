Mr Michael Maitland Stewart , Fulham East

I am sorry to disappoint the highly coloured imagination of my hon. Friend. I think the Committee would not wish me to go into great detail over information already before us in the Army Appropriation Account. But just to give one example, the deficit in the War Office Vote arises from the fact that in the year under discussion the reduction in War Office staff was not as great as had been anticipated; though my hon. Friend will be glad to know that in the subsequent year there was a reduction of some 1,400 persons. The deficit on Vote 6 (Supplies, etc.) arises simply from under-estimation of the amount required for provisions and forage, fuel and light and the underestimates were of the order of 1 per cent., 2 per cent. and 3 per cent., such as is normally expected as a deficiency in estimation. Practically all the items now before us can be explained in those terms of minor errors in Estimates. In the Appropriation Account, they have already come under the eye of the Public Accounts Committee.
